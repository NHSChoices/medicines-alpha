More about salbutamol

How salbutamol works

Salbutamol works by relaxing muscle spasm in the airways of the lungs. This opens up the airways to let air into the lungs more easily.

Taking salbutamol for a long time

Salbutamol is a very safe medicine. There's no evidence that it does any lasting damage to your body even if you use it for many years.

Coming off salbutamol

Don't stop taking salbutamol unless your doctor tells you to.

If you stop taking salbutamol your breathing problems could get worse.

Keep a salbutamol diary

It's a good idea to keep a diary of how often you need to use your salbutamol inhaler. That way you can discuss how it's helping your asthma or COPD with your doctor or nurse.

Keeping a diary will also help you know when to order a replacement inhaler. Some inhalers contain a fixed number of 200 doses and it's not always obvious when they are empty.

Other treatment options

Almost everyone that has asthma or COPD will be prescribed a bronchodilator medicine to help their breathing.

There are two main bronchodilators:

  • salbutamol
  • terbutaline

Salbutamol and terbutaline are very similar in terms of how well they work and their safety but there may be differences in the kind of inhalers they come in.

If you have difficulty using a salbutamol inhaler talk to your doctor. Switching to a terbutaline inhaler may be an option for you.
