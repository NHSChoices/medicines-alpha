Common questions

How long does salbutamol take to work?

Inhaled salbutamol works almost straight away to make breathing easier. It carries on working for about 5 hours.

How long will I need to take salbutamol for?

Once your breathing is better, it may be possible for you or your child to use salbutamol less often.

But you should always have your salbutamol inhaler with you so you can use it as soon as you have breathing problems.

Most people will use salbutamol for many years.

Even if you only have to use salbutamol occasionally, keep your inhaler with you all the time in case you have a sudden attack.

Can I drink alcohol?

Yes, you can drink alcohol with salbutamol.

Can I smoke if I use a salbutamol inhaler?

Try not to smoke. Smoking irritates the lungs and will make your breathing problem worse.

Speak to your GP, nurse or pharmacist if you're finding it hard to stop smoking. There is help available on the NHS.

Can I drive or ride a bike?

Salbutamol can make you feel dizzy or shaky. If this happens, do not drive, cycle or use tools or machinery until you feel better.
