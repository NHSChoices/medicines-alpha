Using your inhaler

Your salbutamol inhaler works almost straight away to make your breathing easier.

It's important that you know how to use your inhaler properly so you're getting all the benefits from your medicine.

Using the inhaler properly

Before using your inhaler, read the manufacturer's printed information leaflet from inside the pack. This leaflet gives you information and diagrams to show you how to use the inhaler, how to keep it clean, and how long to use it before getting a replacement.

It's very important that you use your inhaler properly so you get the right amount of salbutamol into your lungs.

If you're not sure how to use your inhaler, ask your doctor or nurse to show you.

Using a spacer with the inhaler

If you or your child find it difficult to use an inhaler, your doctor may give you a spacer to use with it.

A spacer is a large metal or plastic container with a mouthpiece and a hole for the inhaler. When used with the inhaler it makes it easier to get the right amount of salbutamol into the lungs.

Spacers are especially useful for giving salbutamol to young children. They're also helpful for people who find it difficult to co-ordinate breathing in and pressing an inhaler at the same time.

Your doctor or nurse can show you how to use a spacer with the inhaler.
