Isotretinoin capsules

Isotretinoin capsules are a very effective treatment for severe acne (spots).

This medicine can have serious side effects so it must be prescribed and supervised by a skin specialist.

Isotretinoin also comes as a gel for milder acne.

Isotretinoin capsules are also called by the brand names Roaccutane or Rizuderm.

Treatment with isotretinoin capsules is not suitable for children under 12 or who have not started puberty.

Key facts

  • Isotretinoin capsules are usually taken once or twice a day straight after a meal or snack.
  • Acne normally improves after taking isotretinoin capsules for a week to 10 days.
  • Most people have clear skin after a four to five month course of isotretinoin capsules.
  • The most common side effects of isotretinoin capsules are headaches, aching muscles and joints, dry sensitive skin and skin being sensitive to sunlight.
  • If you are a woman, it’s very important not to become pregnant while using isotretinoin gel or capsules or for at least one month after stopping. This is because isotretinoin can harm an unborn baby.

Check the leaflet inside your medicine packet for specific information about the brand that you are taking.

How and when to take

Isotretinoin is a strong medicine so it's important to take the capsules as instructed by your doctor.

Usually, isotretinoin capsules are taken once or twice a day. Each capsule contains a dose of 10mg or 20mg of isotretinoin.

Swallow the capsules whole with a drink of water. It’s best to take them straight after a meal or snack to make sure they work properly.

How much will I take?

The dose of isotretinoin capsules varies from person to person and depends on your weight.

As a general rule, the starting dose is 0.5 mg per kilogram body weight per day (0.5 mg/kg/day). So if you weigh 60 kg, your dose will usually start at 30 mg a day.

What if I forget to take it?

If you take the capsules once a day and remember you have missed a dose on that day, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it is the next day when you remember, skip the missed dose and have the next dose at the usual time.

If you take the capsules twice a day and forget to take a dose, take it as soon as you remember unless this is within two hours of your next dose, in which case skip the missed dose and take the next dose at the usual time.

Never take a double dose or an extra dose to make up for a missed one.

What if I take too much?

If you accidentally take too many isotretinoin capsules or someone else accidentally takes your medicine, contact your pharmacist, NHS 111, your GP, or your nearest hospital immediately.

Will the amount I take go up or down?

After a few weeks your doctor may change your dose of capsules. For example, you may need a higher dose if your acne is not getting better. If you have any side effects that bother you, your doctor may lower your dose.

Side effects

Like all medicines, isotretinoin capsules can cause side effects although not everyone gets them. Side effects will usually go away when you stop treatment.

The most common side effects of isotretinoin capsules are headaches, aching muscles and joints, dry sensitive skin and skin sensitivity to sunlight.

It has been reported that isotretinoin capsules can cause or worsen depression and even make people feel suicidal. These reports are rare and it’s not clear if the medicine is the cause of the mental health illness or if it was there before. So far there is no clear link between this medicine and depression or suicidal behaviour.

Expand side effects

Common side effects

The common side effects of isotretinoin capsules happen in around one in 10 people. They include:

  • headaches, joint, muscle and back pain
  • dry skin and lips – for safety, don’t have any waxing, dermabrasion or laser skin treatment while you are taking this medicine and for at least six months after you stop as this could cause scarring or skin irritation.
  • skin becoming more sensitive to sunlight – for safety, try to stay out of bright sun and use a high factor sun cream (SPF 15 or above) even on cloudy days. Do not use a sun-lamp or sun-beds.
  • dry eyes
  • dry mouth
  • dry nose and nosebleeds

Serious side effects

Serious side effects are rare and happen in fewer than one in 1,000 people

Stop taking isotretinoin capsules and tell a doctor straight away if you get:

  • depression or mood changes. Symptoms of depression can include anxiety, aggression and violence, changes in mood or suicidal thoughts.
  • stomach problems – severe pain in your tummy, with or without bloody diarrhoea, feeling sick and being sick can be signs of a serious and sometimes life-threatening problem called pancreatitis.
  • a serious skin rash that peels or has blisters. The skin rash may come with eye infections, ulcers, a fever and headaches.
  • muscle weakness – including difficulty moving your arms or legs and painful, swollen or bruised areas of the body. Dark pee can also be a sign of muscle weakness.
  • yellow skin or eyes, difficulty peeing or feeling very tired. These are all signs of liver or kidney problems.
  • a bad headache that does not go away and makes you feel sick or vomit.
  • sudden changes in eyesight including not seeing as well at night

Serious allergic reaction

It's rare to have an allergic reaction to isotretinoin capsules but if this happens to you, stop taking the medicine and contact a doctor straight away.

The warning signs of a serious allergic reaction are:

  • skin rash
  • wheezing
  • tightness in the chest or throat
  • having trouble breathing or talking
  • unusual hoarseness
  • swelling of the mouth, face, lips, tongue, or throat
Info:

These are not all the side effects of isotretinoin capsules. For a full list see the leaflet inside your medicines packet.

Coping with side effects

What to do about headache, joint, muscle and back pain

Ask your pharmacist to recommend a painkiller. Light exercise should not be a problem but try to avoid heavy exercise because it may make joint or muscle pain worse.

What to do about dry skin or lips

Apply a moisturiser and lip balm regularly. The best type of moisturiser is an oil-free face moisturiser for sensitive skin. Try and keep your showers shorter than two minutes and use lukewarm water rather than hot.

What to do about dry eyes

Ask your pharmacist or optician to recommend some eye drops. If you wear contact lenses and these become uncomfortable, you might have to wear glasses instead while you are taking this medicine.

What to do about dry mouth

Try chewing sugar free gum or sweets

What to do about dry nose and nosebleeds

Try applying a thin layer of Vaseline to the inside edges of your nose.

Info:

If you have a side effect from any medicine, including isotretinoin capsules, you can report it using the Yellow Card Scheme.

Mixing with other medicines

Some medicines and isotretinoin capsules can interfere with each other and make it more likely that you will have side effects.

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you’re taking any other medicines, including herbal remedies and supplements, before starting treatment with isotretinoin capsules.

Tell your doctor if you’re taking these medicines before you start treatment with isotretinoin capsules:

  • supplements that contain vitamin A
  • tetracycline antibiotics such as doxycycline, oxytetracycline, minocycline and lymecycline
  • other acne medicines as using both together may make skin irritation worse.
Expand mixing with other medicines

Mixing isotretinoin with complementary remedies

There is very little information about taking herbal and complementary remedies while you use isotretinoin capsules.

For safety, speak to your pharmacist before taking isotretinoin capsules if you routinely take any herbal or alternative remedies.

Common questions

Can I drink alcohol?

Yes, you can drink alcohol with isotretinoin capsules.

When will it start to work?

This can vary. Your acne may get worse when you first start treatment with isotretinoin capsules, but will then normally improve after seven to 10 days.

How long will treatment last?

A treatment course of isotretinoin capsules usually lasts for 16-24 weeks (around four to six months). Acne will normally keep improving for up to eight weeks after stopping. After that time most people have permanently clear skin, but a few will need another round of treatment.

Can I drive during treatment?

Isotretinoin capsules can make you feel sleepy and dizzy. You may also not see as well at night. If this happens to you, don’t drive or use machinery or tools until you have stopped treatment.

Will it affect my fertility?

Isotretinoin capsules do not reduce a man's or woman’s fertility.

Who can take isotretinoin capsules

Isotretinoin capsules contain soya. You should not take this medicine if you have an allergy to this ingredient.

Isotretinoin capsules contain sorbitol. Don't take this medicine if you have an inherited digestive disorder called fructose intolerance.

Isotretinoin capsules are not recommended for children under the age of 12 or before puberty.

Expand who can take isotretenoin capsules

To make sure isotretinoin capsules are safe for you tell your doctor if:

  • you are pregnant or think you could be or you are breastfeeding
  • you have ever had an allergic reaction to isotretinoin or any other medicine
  • you have liver or kidney disease
  • you have high levels of cholesterol or other fats in your blood
  • you have high levels of vitamin A
  • you, or a close family member, have a mental health illness
  • you have inflammatory bowel disease such as Crohn’s disease or ulcerative colitis

If you have diabetes, talk to your doctor before beginning treatment with isotretinoin capsules. You may need extra monitoring while you take isotretinoin capsules as this medicine can cause a rise in blood sugar levels.

Pregnancy and breastfeeding

Isotretinoin capsules are not recommended if you are pregnant or breastfeeding because this medicine can harm a baby.

If you become pregnant during treatment with isotretinoin capsules, stop taking the capsules and tell your doctor as soon as possible.

To prevent pregnancy during treatment with isotretinoin capsules, it's recommended that you use at least one - ideally two - types of contraception for one month before treatment begins, during treatment and for one month after treatment has finished.

Expand pregnancy and breastfeeding

Isotretinoin capsules MUST NOT be used by women during pregnancy as this medicine can cause serious birth defects in the unborn baby.

Women who are able to become pregnant and who are taking isotretinoin capsules must:

  • Use at least one, but ideally two effective methods of contraception without interruption for one month before starting isotretinoin and continue until one month after treatment has stopped. The second contraceptive should be a barrier method of contraception (for example a condom) although you should not use barrier methods on their own. Discuss your contraception with the specialist.
  • Have a pregnancy test before, during and five weeks after the end of treatment. Some doctors may require you to have monthly pregnancy tests.

Men taking isotretinoin capsules

It’s thought that isotretinoin does not damage sperm and that only very low amounts get into semen.

Men can safely take isotretinoin capsules if they and their partner are trying for a baby.

Isotretinoin and breastfeeding

It’s recommended that you should not breastfeed while taking isotretinoin capsules. The medicine is likely to pass into your breast milk and may harm your baby.

More about isotretinoin capsules

How isotretinoin capsules works

Isotretinoin capsules are only for severe acne. This is acne with lots of pus-filled spots that also affects the back and chest as well as the face.

Isotretinoin capsules help severe acne by reducing the amount of natural oil made by your skin so blocked pores are less likely. The medicine also kills the bacteria that cause acne and relieves redness and soreness.

Food and drinks

There are no foods or drinks you need to avoid while taking isotretinoin. You can eat and drink normally.

Other acne medicines

Isotretinoin in the form of capsules is a powerful medicine generally reserved for people whose acne doesn't improve with other treatments.

But there are other options for some people with severe acne, for example treatments that use lasers and other light devices and chemical peels.

Self-help remedies

There are several lifestyle steps that can relieve even severe acne:

  • Try not to wash affected areas of skin more than twice a day. Too much washing can irritate your skin and make spots worse
  • Wash spotty skin with a mild soap or cleanser and lukewarm water. Very hot or cold water can make acne worse.
  • Never ‘clean out’ blackheads or squeeze spots. This can make them worse and cause permanent scars.
  • Don’t use too much make-up and cosmetics. Use water-based products that are described as ‘non-comedogenic’ (this means the product is less likely to block the pores in your skin).
  • Remove make-up before going to bed.
  • Use a fragrance-free, water-based moisturiser if dry skin is a problem
  • Shower as soon as possible after exercising as sweat can irritate acne.
  • Wash your hair regularly and try to stop letting your hair fall across your face.

If you're a blood donor

Don't donate blood while taking isotretinoin capsules and for at least one month after stopping. If your blood is given to a pregnant woman it may harm her unborn baby.
