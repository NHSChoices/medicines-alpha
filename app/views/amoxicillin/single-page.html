Amoxicillin is a type of medicine known as an antibiotic.

It is widely used to treat bacterial infections such as chest infections (including pneumonia), dental abscess and urine infections.

It is used in children, often to treat ear infections and chest infections.

The medicine is available as tablets, capsules or as a liquid that you drink. It is also given by injection, but this is usually only done in hospital.

Amoxicillin is also called by the brand name Amoxil.

Key facts

  • Take amoxicillin three times a day.
  • For most infections you will feel better within a few days.
  • The most common side effects of amoxicillin are feeling sick and diarrhoea.
  • Amoxicillin can cause teeth staining. This is temporary and is removed by brushing.
  • You can drink alcohol while taking amoxicillin.

How and when to take

The usual dose of amoxicillin is 250mg to 500mg taken three times a day. The dose may be lower for children.

Try to space your doses evenly throughout the day. If you take it three times a day this could be first thing in the morning, mid-afternoon and at bedtime. You can take amoxicillin before or after food.

Carry on taking this medicine until you have completed the course even if you feel better. If you stop your treatment early your problem could come back.

More information

Swallow amoxicillin tablets or capsules whole with a drink of water. Do not chew or break them.

Amoxicillin is available as a liquid for children and people who find it difficult to swallow tablets.

If you or your child are taking amoxicillin as a liquid, it will usually be made up for you by your pharmacist. The medicine will come with a dose-measuring plastic syringe or spoon to help you take the right amount. If you don’t have a dose-measuring device, ask your pharmacist for one.

The dose and length of time you need to take it for depends on your age, and the type and severity of the infection you have.

What if I forget to take it?

If you forget to take a dose, take one as soon as you remember, unless it is within 30 to 60 minutes of the next dose. Try to take the correct number of doses each day, leaving at least four hours between doses. Never take two doses at the same time or an extra dose to make up for a forgotten one.

What if I take too much?

Accidentally taking an extra dose of amoxicillin is unlikely to harm you or your child.

Call NHS 111 or speak to your pharmacist or doctor if you are worried or if you take more than one extra dose.

Side effects

Like all medicines, amoxicillin can cause side effects although not everyone gets them. The most common side effects are feeling sick, an upset stomach or diarrhoea.

More information

Common side effects

These common side effects happen in around one in 10 people. Keep taking the medicine, but tell your doctor if they bother you or don't go away:

  • feeling sick
  • being sick
  • diarrhoea
  • upset stomach
  • bloating and indigestion

Serious side effects

Serious side effects are rare and happen in fewer than one in 10,000 people.

Tell a doctor straight away if you get:

  • severe, bloody diarrhoea
  • liver or gall bladder problems: warning signs are yellowing of the whites of the eyes and skin
  • a bad skin rash that may include flushing, fever, blisters or ulcers, or skin that looks like it has been burnt
  • bruising or skin discolouration
  • joint or muscle pain that comes on after two days of taking the medicine
  • skin rash with circular red patches
  • dark urine

Some of these serious side effects can be delayed for up to two months after finishing the course of amoxicillin.

Allergic reactions

It's rare to have a serious allergic reaction to amoxicillin but if this happens to you, stop taking the medicine and contact a doctor straight away.

Around one in 15 people have an allergic reaction to amoxicillin. In most cases the allergic reaction is mild and can take the form of:

  • a raised, itchy skin rash
  • coughing
  • wheezing

Mild allergic reactions can usually be successfully treated by taking antihistamines.

In rare cases, amoxicillin can cause a serious allergic reaction. The warning signs are:

  • getting a skin rash that may include itchy, red, swollen, blistered or peeling skin
  • wheezing
  • tightness in the chest or throat
  • having trouble breathing or talking
  • swelling of the mouth, face, lips, tongue or throat

Serious allergic reaction is a medical emergency. Contact a doctor straight away if you think you or someone around you is having a serious allergic reaction.

These are some of the side effects of amoxicillin. For a full list see the leaflet inside your medicine packet.

Coping with side effects

What to do about feeling sick

Stick to simple meals and don’t eat rich or spicy food while you’re taking this medicine. It might help to take your amoxicillin after a meal or snack.

What to do about diarrhoea and vomiting

Drink plenty of water or other fluids if you have diarrhoea or you are being sick. It may also help to take oral rehydration solutions which you can buy from a pharmacy to prevent dehydration.

Don’t take any other medicines to treat diarrhoea or vomiting without speaking to a pharmacist or doctor.

Info:

If you have a side effect from any medicine, including amoxicillin, you can report it using the Yellow Card Scheme.

Who can take amoxicillin

Amoxicillin isn’t suitable for some people.

To make sure amoxicillin is safe for you, tell your doctor if you:

  • have an allergy to penicillin or amoxicillin
  • have liver or kidney problems
  • have recently had, or are about to have, any vaccinations

Common questions

Can I drink alcohol?

Yes, you can drink alcohol with amoxicillin.

When will I feel better?

For most infections, you should feel better within a few days.

It’s very important that you continue to take amoxicillin until your course is finished, even if you feel better, to stop the infection coming back.

Can I drive?

Yes, amoxicillin should not affect your ability to drive.

Will it stop my contraception working?

No, amoxicillin doesn’t stop contraceptive pills, or any other type of contraceptive, from working.

However, if amoxicillin makes you vomit or have diarrhoea for more than 24 hours, your contraceptive pills (but not other types of contraceptive) may not protect you from pregnancy. If this happens, follow the instructions in the leaflet provided with your contraceptive pills.

Will it give me thrush?

Some people get a fungal infection called thrush after taking a course of antibiotics like amoxicillin. Antibiotics kill the normal harmless bacteria which help to defend against thrush. Symptoms include redness and itching in the mouth. Women may get vaginal itching. If this happens to you, speak to your doctor or pharmacist for advice.

Will it affect my fertility?

Amoxicillin will not affect your fertility, but speak to a pharmacist or your doctor before taking it if you are trying to get pregnant.

Taking amoxicillin with other medicines

There are some medicines that do not mix well with amoxicillin and can change the way it works.

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking any other medicines, including herbal remedies and supplements, before starting amoxicillin.

More information

Tell your doctor if you're taking these medicines before you start taking amoxicillin treatment:

  • methotrexate
  • a blood thinner called warfarin
  • gout medicines called probenecid and allopurinol
  • other antibiotics

Mixing amoxicillin with complementary remedies

There are no known problems with taking herbal and complementary remedies with amoxicillin.

For safety, speak to your pharmacist before taking amoxicillin if you routinely take any herbal or alternative remedies.

Pregnancy and breastfeeding

Amoxicillin can generally be taken at the usual doses during pregnancy and while breastfeeding.

For safety, tell your doctor if you are pregnant or breastfeeding before starting amoxicillin.

More information

Breastfeeding

Amoxicillin passes into breast milk but it’s not linked with any major side effects in breastfed babies.

More about amoxicillin

How amoxicillin works

Amoxicillin is a similar antibiotic to penicillin. It works by killing the bacteria causing the infection.

Tooth staining

If you or your child take amoxicillin as a liquid medicine, you may find that it causes some teeth staining. This is temporary and should go after brushing your teeth well.

If symptoms don't improve

Tell your doctor if you don’t start feeling better after taking amoxicillin for three days, or at any time if you start to feel worse.

Food and drink to avoid

There are no food or drinks you need to avoid while taking amoxicillin.

Non-medical treatment options

There are no non-medical options available for treating infections.
