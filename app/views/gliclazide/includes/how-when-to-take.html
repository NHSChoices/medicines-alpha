How and when to take

The dose of gliclazide can vary. Take this medicine as prescribed by your doctor.

It’s usual to take the tablets once a day before breakfast.

If you’re on a higher dose you may take gliclazide tablets up to four times a day. Try to always take them before a meal.

Swallow your gliclazide tablets whole with a glass of water, do not chew them.

More information

Gliclazide comes as two different types of tablets – normal (standard release) and long-acting (extended release).

Standard release tablets release gliclazide into your body quickly so you may need to take them several times a day depending on your dose.

Extended release tablets dissolve slowly which means you don’t have to take them as regularly as the standard ones, one dose in the morning is usually enough.

Your doctor or pharmacist will explain what type of gliclazide tablets you are on and how to take them.

How much will I take?

For extended release gliclazide, the maximum daily dose is 120mg taken once a day before breakfast.

For standard release gliclazide, the maximum daily dose is 320mg which may be split into up to four doses. Don’t take more than 160mg as a single dose and try to take the tablets before a meal so your blood sugar level doesn’t go too low.

Will my dose go up or down?

Your doctor will check your blood sugar levels regularly and may adjust your dose of gliclazide if necessary.

What if I forget to take it?

If you miss a dose of gliclazide, take the next dose at the usual time. Do not take a double dose to make up for a forgotten dose.

What if I take too much?

If you take too many gliclazide tablets, contact your doctor or the nearest hospital straight away. An overdose of gliclazide can cause low blood sugar.

If you think you have some of these symptoms, have some food or drink that quickly gets sugar into your bloodstream such as sugar cubes or fruit juice. You may also need to eat a starchy carbohydrate, like a sandwich or a biscuit.
