Side effects

Like all medicines, gliclazide can cause side effects although not everyone gets them.

The most common side effects of gliclazide are tummy upsets. If they happen, side effects are usually mild and happen in fewer than one in 10 people.

More information

Common side effects

These common side effects happen in up to one in 10 people:

  • stomach ache
  • feeling sick
  • vomiting
  • indigestion
  • diarrhoea
  • constipation

Side effects can be less if you take gliclazide tablets before a meal.

Serious side effects

These serious side effects are rare and affect fewer than one in 10,000 people.

Tell your doctor straight away if you get:

  • liver disorder - warning signs include yellowing of the skin or whites of the eye
  • blood disorder - warning signs include paleness, prolonged bleeding, bruising, sore throat and fever
  • skin disorders such as rash, redness, itching, hives; you can also have rapid swelling of eyelids, face, lips, mouth, tongue or throat that may result in breathing difficulty
  • eye disorders - your vision may be affected for a short time especially at the start of treatment due to changes in your blood sugar levels

Low blood sugar

Gliclazide can sometimes cause your blood sugar to go too low. The medical name for this is hypoglycaemia. Early warning signs of low blood sugar include:

  • feeling hungry
  • trembling or shaking
  • sweating
  • confusion
  • difficulty concentrating

It’s also possible for your blood sugar to go too low while you’re asleep. If this happens, it can cause make you feel sweaty, tired and confused when you wake up.

Low blood sugar may happen if you:

  • eat meals irregularly or skip meals
  • are fasting
  • don’t eat a healthy diet with enough nutrients in
  • change what you eat
  • increase your physical activity without eating more to compensate
  • drink alcohol, especially after skipping a meal
  • take other medicines or natural remedies at the same time
  • take too much gliclazide
  • suffer from a hormone disorder such as hypothyroidism
  • have kidney or liver problems

What to do about low blood sugar

Have regular meals, including breakfast, and never miss or delay a meal.

If you are planning to increase your physical activity, eat carbohydrates (bread, pasta, cereals) before, during or after exercise.

Always carry a rapid-acting carbohydrate with you, like sugar cubes, fruit juice, or some sweets in case your blood sugar level gets low. Artificial sweeteners won’t help. You may also need to eat a starchy carbohydrate, like a sandwich or a biscuit

If taking sugar does not help or if the symptoms come back, contact your doctor or the nearest hospital.

Make sure your friends and family know about your diabetes and the symptoms of low blood sugar level.

These are not all the side effects of gliclazide. For a full list see the leaflet inside your medicine packet.

How to cope with side effects

What to do about feeling sick

Take your tablets after a meal (should we say this when it may mean your blood sugar drops? Plus it contradicts what we’ve said before – perhaps leave out?). Stick to simple meals and avoid rich or spicy food.

What to do about diarrhoea and vomiting

Drink plenty of water if you have diarrhoea or you are being sick – have small, frequent sips. It may also help to take oral rehydration solutions which you can buy from a pharmacy or supermarket to prevent dehydration.

Don’t take any other medicines to treat diarrhoea or vomiting without speaking to a pharmacist or doctor.

What to do about constipation

If you have constipation, eat more high-fibre foods such as fresh fruit and vegetables and cereals, and drink plenty of water. Also exercise more regularly, for example, by going for a daily walk or run. If this does not help, talk to your pharmacist or doctor.

What to do about weight gain

Gliclazide can make you hungrier and retain water so it can be quite challenging to keep from putting on weight while you're taking it. Try to eat a healthy balanced diet without increasing your portion sizes so you don't gain too much weight. Regular exercise will also help to keep your weight stable.

Info:

If you have a side effect from any medicine, including gliclazide, you can report it using the Yellow Card Scheme.
