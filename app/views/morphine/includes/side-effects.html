Side effects

Like all medicines, morphine can cause side effects in some people, but many people have no side effects or only minor ones.

The higher the dose of morphine the more chance that you will get side effects.

Common side effects

Common side effects happen in more than 1 in 100 people. They generally get better after youâ€™ve been taking morphine for a few days. Tell your doctor if they bother you or don't go away. Common side effects include:

  • constipation
  • feeling and being sick
  • dizziness and vertigo (a sensation of spinning)
  • confusion
  • feeling sleepy
  • headache
  • itchiness and rash

Serious side effects

Serious side effects are rare and happen in less than 1 in 10,000 people. Tell your doctor if you get:

  • heart problems
  • seizures
  • breathing difficulty or short shallow breathing
  • muscle stiffness
  • low blood pressure which can make you feel dizzy, tired, and have low energy

Serious allergic reaction

The warning signs of a serious allergic reaction are:

  • getting a skin rash that may include itchy, red, swollen, blistered or peeling skin
  • wheezing
  • tightness in the chest or throat
  • having trouble breathing or talking
  • swelling of the mouth, face, lips, tongue or throat

Serious allergic reaction is a medical emergency. Contact a doctor straight away if you think you or someone else is having a serious allergic reaction.

These are not all the side effects of morphine. For a full list see the leaflet inside your medicine packet.
