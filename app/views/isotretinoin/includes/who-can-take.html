Who can take isotretinoin

Isotretinoin capsules (but not isotretinoin gel) contain soya. You should not take this medicine if you have an allergy to this ingredient.

Isotretinoin capsules (but not isotretinoin gel) contain sorbitol. You should not take this medicine if you have an inherited digestive disorder called fructose intolerance.

Isotretinoin gel may not be suitable if you get eczema or you have had skin cancer.

Isotretinoin capsules or gel are not recommended for children under the age of 12 or before puberty.

To make sure isotretinoin capsules are safe for you tell your doctor if:

  • you are pregnant or think you could be or you are breastfeeding
  • you have ever had an allergic reaction to isotretinoin or any other medicine
  • you have liver or kidney disease
  • you have high levels of cholesterol or other fats in your blood
  • you have high levels of vitamin A
  • you, or a close family member, have a mental health illness
  • you have inflammatory bowel disease such as Crohnâ€™s disease or ulcerative colitis

To make sure isotretinoin gel is safe for you tell your doctor if:

  • you are pregnant or think you could be or you are breastfeeding
  • you have eczema
  • you or close family have had skin cancer

If you have diabetes, talk to your doctor before beginning treatment with isotretinoin capsules. You may need extra monitoring while you take isotretinoin capsules as this medicine can cause a rise in blood sugar levels. Isotretinoin gel does not affect blood sugar levels.
