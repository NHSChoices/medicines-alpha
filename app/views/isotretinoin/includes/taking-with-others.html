Taking with other medicines

Some medicines and isotretinoin can interfere with each other and make it more likely that you will have side effects.

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you’re taking any other medicines, including herbal remedies and supplements, before starting treatment with isotretinoin capsules or gel.

Tell your doctor if you’re taking these medicines before your start isotretinoin treatment:

Isotretinoin capsules

  • supplements that contain vitamin A
  • tetracycline antibiotics such as doxycycline, oxytetracycline, minocycline and lymecycline
  • other acne medicines as using both together may make skin irritation worse.

Isotretinoin gel

  • other acne medicines as using both together may make skin irritation worse
  • benzoyl peroxide cream or gel (another acne treatment) as it may make isotretinoin gel less effective.

Mixing isotretinoin with complementary remedies

There is very little information about taking herbal and complementary remedies with isotretinoin capsules or gel.

For safety, speak to your pharmacist before taking isotretinoin if you routinely take any herbal or alternative remedies.
