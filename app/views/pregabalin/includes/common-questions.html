Common questions

Can I drink alcohol?

You can drink alcohol with pregabalin but it may make you feel sleepy or make you lose your focus. During the first few days of taking pregabalin, it might be best to stop drinking alcohol until you see how the medicine affects you.

Can I drive or ride a bike?

You may feel sleepy, tired or dizzy when you first start taking pregabalin. This can also happen if your dose has increased. If this happens to you, don't drive or ride a bike until you feel more alert.

If you have epilepsy, you are not allowed to drive until you have had no seizures for 1 year, or if you only have seizures while you are asleep. You are also not supposed to drive if your epilepsy medicine has changed and for 6 months after.

When will I feel better?

It takes at least a few weeks for pregabalin to work properly. You may still have seizures or pain during this time.

Are there similar medicines to pregabalin?

Gabapentin (also called Neurontin) is a medicine that works in a similar way to pregabalin. Like pregabalin, it is taken to treat epilepsy and nerve pain. It is also be taken for migraines.

However, there are other differences between pregabalin and gabapentin. Gabapentin is taken in different doses to pregabalin.

If you need to change to gabapentin treatment, your doctor will explain how to safely swap from pregabalin.

Is it safe to take for a long time?

There's no evidence that pregabalin has lasting harmful effects even if you take it for many months or years.
