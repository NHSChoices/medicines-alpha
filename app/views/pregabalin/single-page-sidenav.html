How and when to take

To prevent side effects, your doctor will prescribe a low dose to start with and then increase it over a few days.

Swallow pregabalin capsules whole with a drink of water or juice. Do not chew them.

If you are taking pregabalin as a liquid, it will come with a syringe or spoon to measure your dose. If you don’t have a syringe or spoon, ask your pharmacist for one.

Will my dose go up or down?

Pregabalin capsules come in a variety of different strengths.

The dose of pregabalin you have been prescribed depends on your illness, your age, how well your kidneys work, and how severe your symptoms are.

When you first start taking pregabalin your dose might be gradually increased. This is to let your body get used to it and find a dose that works well for you.

Once you find a dose that suits you your dose will not usually change any more.

How long will I take it for?

If you have epilepsy, it is likely that once your illness is under control you will continue to take pregabalin for many years.

If you have nerve pain or anxiety it is likely that once your symptoms have gone you will continue to take pregabalin for several months to stop them coming back.

What if I forget to take it?

If you forget a dose, take it as soon as you remember. If it is within 2 hours of the next dose, it is better to leave out the missed dose and take your next dose as normal.

Never take two doses at the same time. Never take an extra dose to make up for a forgotten one.

If you have epilepsy, it’s important to take the medicine regularly as recommended by your doctor. Missing doses may trigger a seizure. If you forget doses often, it may help to get a special pillbox or a watch with an alarm to remind you.

What if I take too much?

Taking too much pregabalin by accident may cause unpleasant side effects. These include feeling sleepy, confused or agitated, seizures, and passing out.

If this happens, call your doctor straight away, or go to the nearest hospital accident and emergency department.

Take with you any pregabalin that you have left, and the labelled container so the hospital can see what you have taken.
