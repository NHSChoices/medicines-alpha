Side effects

Like all medicines, naproxen can cause side effects although not everyone gets them.

Common side effects

Common side effects of naproxen happen in more than 1 in 100 people. They include:

  • confusion
  • headache
  • ringing in the ears
  • changes in vision
  • tiredness, feeling sleepy
  • dizziness
  • rashes

Less common side effects of naproxen happen in more than 1 in 1000 people. They include:

  • depression
  • irregular heartbeat (palpitations)
  • abnormal dreams
  • forgetfulness
  • difficulty concentrating
  • sensitivity of the skin to light (may cause blistering)
  • difficulty sleeping

Rarely, naproxen can cause some side effects that happen in less than 1 in 1000 people:

  • hair loss
  • problems with hearing
  • inflammation of blood vessels - causing fever, swelling and generally not feeling well
  • asthma getting worse
  • muscle weakness and pain
  • ulcers on the inner cheeks, gums and tongue

Serious side effects

Tell your doctor straight away if you have:

  • a serious allergic reaction - warning signs include a rash, swelling of the face, mouth, tongue, difficulty breathing or wheezing
  • ulcer or inflammation in the stomach or gut - warning signs include severe indigestion, heartburn, pains in your stomach, feeling sick or vomiting, diarrhoea
  • bleeding and perforation of the stomach and gut - warning signs include vomiting blood or dark particles that look like coffee grounds, blood in your poo or black tarry looking poo
  • abnormalities in your blood cells (agranulocytosis) - warning signs include frequent sore throat, nose bleeds, and infections
  • anaemia - warning signs include fainting, chest pain, breathlessness
  • aseptic meningitis - warning signs include fever, feeling sick or vomiting, confusion, headache, neck stiffness and sensitivity to light
  • Stevens-Johnson syndrome - warning signs include severe skin rash with flushing, blisters or ulcers
  • kidney damage or infection - warning signs include blood in your pee, decrease in amount of pee passed, feeling sick or vomiting
  • jaundice or inflammation of the liver - warning signs include feeling tired, loss of appetite, feeling sick or vomiting, pale coloured poo and yellowing of the skin or whites of the eyes.
  • irregular, slow heart beats caused by high levels of potassium in the blood.
  • inflammation of the pancreas - warning signs include fever, stomach pain and vomiting.

These are not all the side effects of naproxen. For a full list see the leaflet inside your medicine packet.
