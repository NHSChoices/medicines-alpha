Side effects

Like all medicines, metformin can cause side effects although not everyone gets them.

Common side effects

These common side effects happen in up to one in 10 people:

  • feeling sick
  • vomiting
  • diarrhoea
  • stomach ache
  • loss of appetite
  • metallic taste in the mouth

Tell your doctor if they bother you or don't go away.

Serious side effects

These serious side effects are rare and affect less than one in 10,000 people.

Tell your doctor straight away if you get:

  • lactic acidosis - warning signs come on quickly and include a general feeling of discomfort with severe tiredness, fast or shallow breathing, being cold and a slow heartbeat
  • inflammation of the liver - warning signs include tiredness, loss of appetite, weight loss and yellowing of the skin or whites of the eyes
  • a skin rash, redness or itching
  • anaemia - warning signs include extreme tiredness, lack of energy, pins and needles, a sore and red tongue, mouth ulcers, muscle weakness and disturbed vision

These are not all the side effects of metformin. For a full list see the leaflet inside your medicine packet.
