Key facts

  • Your child should start to feel less pain about 30 minutes after taking paracetamol tablets or syrup. It may take up to an hour for a suppository to work properly.
  • Paracetamol comes in a range of different strengths. The right dose for your child depends on their age and weight.
  • Do not give your child paracetamol with other medicines containing paracetamol.
  • Paracetamol is an everyday medicine, but it can be dangerous if too much is taken. Be careful to keep it out of the reach of children.
  • Keep the medicine in a cupboard, away from heat and sunlight. It does not need to be kept in the fridge.

Check the leaflet inside your medicine packet for information about the brand that your child is taking.
