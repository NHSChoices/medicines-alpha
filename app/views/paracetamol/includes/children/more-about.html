More about paracetamol

How paracetamol works

Paracetamol seems to work by blocking chemical messengers in the brain that tell children that they have pain.

Paracetamol reduces fever by affecting an area of the brain that regulates their body temperature.

When will they feel better?

Paracetamol tablets and syrup take about half an hour to work. Suppositories take around an hour to work.

If your child's pain lasts for more than 3 days, see your doctor.

Food and drinks

Your child can eat and drink normally while taking paracetamol.

You can give your child paracetamol (but not ibuprofen) on an empty stomach.

Can you give paracetamol with prescription medicines?

In general, paracetamol doesn't interfere with prescription medicines including antibiotics.

However, paracetamol isn't suitable for some children. Talk to your doctor if they take:

  • warfarin (a blood-thinning medicine)
  • medicine to treat epilepsy
  • medicine to treat tuberculosis (TB)

Can you give paracetamol with herbal remedies or supplements?

Check with your doctor or pharmacist before giving any herbal remedies or supplements to your child.
