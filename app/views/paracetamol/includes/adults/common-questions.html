Common questions

Can I drink alcohol?

Drinking a small amount of alcohol (no more than 14 units a week) while taking paracetamol is usually safe.

It may not be safe for you to drink alcohol with paracetamol if you have certain health conditions, such as liver problems. Check the leaflet in your medicine box to find out whether it's safe to drink while you're taking paracetamol.

Can I take paracetamol with other painkillers?

You can safely take paracetamol with other types of painkiller (that don't contain paracetamol) such as ibuprofen, aspirin and codeine, if your pain isn't controlled by paracetamol alone.

Don't give children other painkillers, including liquid ibuprofen, at the same time as paracetamol. Never give aspirin to children under 16.

Can I take paracetamol with my other medicines?

Do not take paracetamol if you are also taking other medicines that contain paracetamol, such as codeine combinations, migraine preparations and some cough and cold remedies, because of the risk of overdosing on paracetamol. This may damage your liver and can be fatal.

Can I take herbal remedies with paracetamol?

Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you're taking the herbal medicine St John's Wort for depression as you may need to reduce your paracetamol dose.

Otherwise, paracetamol is not generally affected by also taking herbal remedies or supplements.

Are there any foods or drinks I should avoid?

You can eat and drink normally while taking paracetamol.
