Common questions

Is paracetamol or ibuprofen better?

Paracetamol and ibuprofen are similar strengths but they work in different ways. So paracetamol is better for some types of pain than ibuprofen.

Paracetamol is usually best for most types of pain, including headache, stomach ache and nerve pain such as sciatica.

Ibuprofen is better for period pain or toothache. Some people find ibuprofen better than paracetamol for back pain.

Can I drink alcohol with paracetamol?

Drinking a small amount of alcohol (no more than 14 units a week) while taking paracetamol is usually safe.

It may not be safe for you to drink alcohol with paracetamol if you have certain health conditions, such as liver problems. Check the leaflet in your medicine packet to find out whether it's safe to drink while you're taking paracetamol.

Can I take herbal remedies with paracetamol?

Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you're taking the herbal medicine St John's Wort for depression as you may need to reduce your paracetamol dose.

Otherwise, paracetamol is not generally affected by also taking herbal remedies or supplements.

Can I take paracetamol with antibiotics?

It's safe to take paracetamol while you are taking antibiotics.

Is there any food or drink to avoid?

You can eat and drink normally while taking paracetamol.

You can safely take paracetamol (but not ibuprofen) on an empty stomach.
