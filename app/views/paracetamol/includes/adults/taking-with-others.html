Taking paracetamol with other medicines

In general, paracetamol does not interfere with other medicines.

It's important, though, that you do not take another medicine with paracetamol in at the same time because of the risk of overdosing on paracetamol.

Paracetamol isn't suitable for some people. Talk to your doctor if you take:

  • warfarin (paracetamol can increase the risk of bleeding)
  • medicine to treat epilepsy
  • medicine to treat tuberculosis (TB)

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if you are taking a medicine, including ones you buy like herbal remedies and supplements, before taking paracetamol.

Other medicines containing paracetamol

It's safe to take paracetamol with most medicines including antibiotics.

It's important, though, that you do not take another medicine with paracetamol in at the same time because of the risk of overdosing on paracetamol.

Paracetamol is an ingredient in many pharmacy cold and flu remedies, such as Lemsip and Night Nurse.

Paracetamol may also be combined with other painkillers which you may have been prescribed by your doctor such as:

  • co-codamol (paracetamol and codeine)
  • co-dydramol (paracetamol and dihydrocodeine)
  • tramacet (paracetamol and tramadol)

Before taking any other medicines, check the label to see whether they contain paracetamol.

Mixing paracetamol with herbal remedies and supplements

Check with your doctor or pharmacist if you're taking the herbal medicine St John's Wort for depression as you may need to reduce your paracetamol dose.

Otherwise, paracetamol is not generally affected by herbal remedies or supplements.
