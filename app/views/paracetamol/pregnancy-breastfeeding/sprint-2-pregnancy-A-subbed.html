Paracetamol

Taking paracetamol when you're pregnant or breastfeeding

In pregnancy:

  • it's safe to take paracetamol in pregnancy

It's natural to be concerned about how your medicines will affect your baby but evidence suggests that paracetamol is safe for you and your unborn baby provided you take it in the recommended doses.

Although it's not possible to say that any medicine is absolutely safe in pregnancy, paracetamol has been used for many years by pregnant women without any obvious harmful effects on the baby.

For this reason, it's usually recommended as the first choice of painkiller for pregnant women.

While breastfeeding:

  • it's safe to take paracetamol while you're breastfeeding

Although paracetamol passes into breast milk, it's not in large enough amounts to have any effect on your baby.
