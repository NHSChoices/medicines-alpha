Prednisolone

Prednisolone: what it is and what it's used for

Prednisolone is one of a group of medicines known as corticosteroids or simply 'steroids'.

Corticosteroids aren't the same as anabolic steroids which are used to treat a completely different set of health conditions and are known for being taken by body builders to boost muscle.

Corticosteroids are produced naturally in the body and help control our response to stress, regulate blood electrolyte levels and control our metabolism.

Prednisolone and other corticosteroids are very important medicines because they can help suppress the immune system and reduce inflammation.

What is prednisolone used for?

Prednisolone is used to treat a wide range of health conditions including:

  • allergies
  • inflammatory diseases - for example affecting the eyes, bowels (ulcerative colitis and Crohn's disease), joints (arthritis) or lungs (asthma)
  • some skin diseases (psoriasis and eczema)
  • some infections
  • certain types of cancer
  • to prevent organ rejection after a transplant

Prednisolone must be prescribed by a doctor. It's available as tablets, an oral solution and as eye and ear drops. It can also be given by injection but this is usually only done in hospital.

It may be called by one of many brand names including Deltacortril, Deltastab, Dilacort and Pevanti.
