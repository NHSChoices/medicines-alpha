Prednisolone

Stopping prednisolone

If you've taken prednisolone for more than three weeks or have taken more than 40mg every day for more than a week it's very important that you do not go 'cold turkey' and stop taking the medicine suddenly.

This is because:

  • suddenly stopping the medicine may lead to your condition flaring up again even though it appears to be under control
  • you may have dangerous withdrawal symptoms such as severe fatigue, weakness, body aches and joint pain

Prednisolone is similar to cortisol, a hormone naturally made by your adrenal glands. If you take prednisolone for more than a few weeks, your adrenal glands decrease cortisol production.

To prevent withdrawal symptoms, gradually reduce the prednisolone dose over a few weeks. By tapering the dose in this way your adrenal glands have time to resume their normal function.
