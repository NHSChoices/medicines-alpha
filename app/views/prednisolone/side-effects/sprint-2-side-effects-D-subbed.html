Prednisolone

Possible side effects

Like all medicines, prednisolone can cause side effects in some people but don't worry, not everyone gets them.

You're more likely to experience side effects if you take prednisolone as tablets and in a higher dose (more than 10mg/day).

Some side effects such as mood changes or stomach problems can happen straight away. Others, like weight gain, only happen after weeks or months.

If you feel unwell while taking prednisolone, keep taking your tablets, but tell your doctor straight away.

Common side effects

Tell your doctor if these common side effects bother you or do not go away:

  • upset stomach, indigestion, feeling sick
  • not able to sleep
  • restlessness
  • sweating a lot
  • significant weight gain

Common and serious side effects

Mood changes are a common, potentially serious side effect of prednisolone. Tell your doctor straight away if you experience:

  • feeling depressed (including having suicidal thoughts)
  • feeling anxious
  • feeling elated or 'high'
  • having strange or frightening thoughts

Rare but serious side effects

Continue taking your medication but tell a doctor straight away if you experience any of these serious side effects:

  • infections: warning signs include fever, chills, very sore throat, ear or sinus pain, cough, more sputum or change in colour of sputum, pain with passing urine, mouth sores, or wound that will not heal
  • high blood sugar levels: warning signs include confusion, feeling sleepy, increased thirst, increased hunger, passing urine more often, flushing, fast breathing, or breath that smells like fruit
  • development of Cushing's disease: warning signs include weight gain in the upper back or belly, moon face, very bad headache, and slow wound healing
  • adrenal gland problems: warning signs include a very upset stomach or vomiting, very bad dizziness or passing out, muscle weakness, feeling very tired, mood changes, loss of appetite and weight loss
  • low potassium levels: warning signs include muscle pain or weakness, muscle cramps, or a heartbeat that does not feel normal
  • pancreas problems: warning signs include severe stomach pain, severe back pain, or severe upset stomach or vomiting
  • any of the following: Shortness of breath, significant weight gain, swelling in the arms or legs, skin changes (pimples, stretch marks, slow healing, hair growth), chest pain or pressure, period (menstrual) changes, bone or joint pain, change in eyesight, change in the way you behave, seizures, burning, numbness, or tingling feeling that is not normal, any bruising or bleeding that is not normal, black, tarry, or bloody stools, vomiting blood or vomit that looks like coffee grounds
