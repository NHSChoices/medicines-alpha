How and when to take

Children can take ibuprofen as:

  • a syrup - from the age of 3 months
  • tablets - from the age of 6 years
  • granules – from the age of 12 years

When to give ibuprofen

Ibuprofen is usually given 3 or 4 times a day. Your pharmacist or doctor will tell you how often to give it.

If you give it:

  • 3 times a day, leave at least 6 hours between doses
  • 4 times a day, leave at least 4 hours between doses

If your child has pain all the time, your doctor may prescribe modified-release tablets or capsules. These are given once or twice a day. Your doctor will tell you how often to give the medicine.

Give ibuprofen with food or milk to reduce stomach upset.

How much ibuprofen to give

Ibuprofen tablets, capsules, syrup and granules come in a range of strengths. Children need to take a lower dose than adults, depending on their age.

As a general rule, babies and children can have ibuprofen syrup up to age 12 and beyond. While only children aged 6 and older can have tablets. Granules are only for children aged 12 and older.

If you are not sure how much to give, ask your pharmacist or doctor.

Ibuprofen syrup dosages for children

Age How much
Babies 3 - 6 months weighing more than 5kg One 2.5ml dose may be taken 3 times in 24 hours
Babies 6 - 12 months weighing more than 5kg One 2.5ml dose may be taken 3 to 4 times in 24 hours
Children 1 - 3 years One 5ml dose may be taken 3 times in 24 hours
Children 4 - 6 years 7.5ml (5ml + 2.5ml spoonful) may be taken 3 times in 24 hours
Children 7 - 9 years Two 5ml doses may be taken 3 times in 24 hours
Children 10 - 12 years Three 5ml doses may be taken 3 times in 24 hours

Ibuprofen tablet dosages for children

Age How much
Child 7–10 years 200 mg 3 times daily
Child 10–12 years 300 mg 3 times daily
Child 12–18 years Initially 300–400 mg 3–4 times daily;
increased if necessary to max. 600 mg 4 times daily

It’s best to give your child ibuprofen with or just after food to reduce their chance of getting an upset stomach.

How to give ibuprofen to a child

Syrup

Shake the bottle well and measure out the right amount using a plastic syringe or spoon. These come in the medicine packet. If you don't have a syringe or spoon, ask your pharmacist for one. Do not use a kitchen teaspoon as it will not give the right amount.

To hide the taste of liquid medicine, you can give the child a drink of milk or fruit juice straight after giving the medicine.

Tablets and capsules

Tablets and capsules should be swallowed whole with a glass of water or juice. Tell your child not to chew, break, crush or suck them as this could irritate their mouth or throat.

Granules

Sprinkle or stir the granules into a small amount of soft food (such as yogurt) or a small drink, or you can mix them with a spoonful of cold water. Do not mix the granules with warm food or liquid. Your child should then swallow the food or drink it straight away, without chewing. Make sure that they take it all. Do not keep the granule/food mixture to give later.

What if I forget to give it?

Give the missed dose as soon as you remember, unless it is almost time for their next dose. In this instance, skip the missed dose and give their next dose as usual.

Never give a double dose to make up for a forgotten one.

What if they take too much?

Giving your child too much ibuprofen by accident can be dangerous.

If you think you may have given your child an extra dose of ibuprofen by mistake, wait at least 12 hours before giving them any more.

If your child may have had more than a double dose of ibuprofen, take them to hospital straight away.

It can be helpful to take any remaining medicine and the box or leaflet with you to hospital if you can.
