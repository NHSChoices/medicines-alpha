Side effects in children

Ibuprofen can cause side effects. To reduce the chance of side effects, give your child the lowest dose for the shortest time to control their symptoms.

Common side effects

The common side effects of ibuprofen, which happen in more than one in 100 children, are stomach pain, indigestion or heartburn. Your child may also feel sick or vomit. You can help to reduce the chances of this by giving ibuprofen with food.

If these symptoms carry on, or get worse, they may be signs of irritation to the gut stomach. Contact your doctor if this happens.

Your child may also get diarrhoea or they may hear ringing in their ears.

Serious side effects

  • If your child is short of breath or is wheezing, or their face, lips or tongue start to swell, or they develop a rash, they may be having a serious allergic reaction to ibuprofen. Take your child to hospital or call an ambulance straight away.
  • If your child gets bad stomach pains, vomits blood or their poo are very dark or black, contact your doctor or take your child to hospital straight away, as they may have a stomach ulcer.
  • Ibuprofen may make asthma worse in some children, but this is uncommon. Contact your doctor if you are worried.
  • If your child stops peeing or there is blood in their pee, contact your doctor straight away. There may be a problem with their kidneys.
