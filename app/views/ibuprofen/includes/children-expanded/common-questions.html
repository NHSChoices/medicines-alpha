Common questions

How does ibuprofen work?

Ibuprofen is a type of medicine called a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID). It works by reducing hormones that cause pain and swelling in the body.

How long will it take to work?

Your child should start to feel less pain about 20 to 30 minutes after taking ibuprofen.

If they need to take it for more than 3 days, or their symptoms do not improve or get worse, talk to a doctor.

What if my child vomits?

If your child vomits less than 30 minutes after having a dose of ibuprofen, give them the same dose again.

If your child vomits more than 30 minutes after having a dose of tablets of ibuprofen, you do not need to give them another dose. Wait until the next normal dose.

Can I give other painkillers?

If ibuprofen does not seem to be helping your child’s pain, try alternating doses of ibuprofen with doses of paracetamol (Calpol) at 2 to 3 hour intervals.

Do not give extra doses of ibuprofen. Do not give another medicine that contains ibuprofen as you risk giving too much ibuprofen.

Do not give aspirin to children under the age of 16.

Can I give ibuprofen with prescription medicines?

Ibuprofen should not be taken with some prescription medicines. Check with your doctor or pharmacist before giving any other medicines to your child.

Tell your doctor or pharmacist if your child is taking any other medicine, including herbal remedies and supplements.

Is there any food or drink to avoid?

Your child can eat and drink normally while taking ibuprofen.

It is best to give ibuprofen with, or just after, a meal, so that it does not upset your child’s stomach. Do not give it on an empty stomach.
