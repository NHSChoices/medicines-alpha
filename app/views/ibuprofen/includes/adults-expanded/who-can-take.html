Who can take ibuprofen

Some brands of ibuprofen contain aspartame, colours (E-numbers), gelatin, glucose, lactose, sodium, sorbitol, soya or sucrose, so may be unsuitable for some people.

To make sure ibuprofen is safe for you, tell your doctor or pharmacist if you:

  • have previously had an allergic reaction to ibuprofen
  • have had bleeding in your stomach, a stomach ulcer, or perforation (a hole) in your stomach
  • have a health problem that means you have an increased chance of bleeding
  • have liver problems, such as liver fibrosis, cirrhosis or liver failure
  • have heart disease or severe heart failure
  • have kidney failure

  • have chickenpox or shingles, as taking ibuprofen can increase the chance of certain infections and skin reactions
