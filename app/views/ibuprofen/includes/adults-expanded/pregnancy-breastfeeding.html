Pregnancy and breastfeeding

Ibuprofen is not normally recommended in pregnancy, especially if you are 30 or more weeks pregnant. Research has shown it may cause birth defects affecting the heart or blood vessels.

There may also be a link between taking ibuprofen in early pregnancy and miscarriage.

Paracetamol is the best painkiller to take during pregnancy.

Ibuprofen is safe to take by mouth or use on your skin if you are breastfeeding as only very small amounts get into breast milk.

Tell your doctor if you plan to get pregnant or are already pregnant before taking ibuprofen.
