About citalopram

What citalopram does

Citalopram is a type of antidepressant medicine that is widely used to treat depression and also sometimes for panic attacks.

Citalopram is one of a group of antidepressants called selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors, or SSRIs, which are thought to work by increasing the levels of a mood-enhancing chemical called serotonin in the brain.

Citalopram is available on prescription as tablets and as oral drops for people that have problems swallowing tablets.

Its brand name is Cipramil.

Ask your doctor before taking citalopram

  • If you have an allergy to citalopram or any of its ingredients. Citalopram tablets contain a small amount of lactose
  • If you are taking any other medicines, including any complementary medicines. Citalopram may not be suitable for you if you already take other medicines which affect your heart rhythm
  • If you're trying to become pregnant, are already pregnant or breast feeding
  • If you have diabetes citalopram can upset its control. Monitor your blood glucose more often for the first few weeks of treatment with citalopram and adjust your diabetes treatment if necessary
