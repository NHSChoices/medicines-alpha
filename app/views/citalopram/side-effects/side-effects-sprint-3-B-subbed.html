Citalopram

Side effects

Tell a doctor straight away if you experience these potentially serious side effects:

  • serotonin syndrome: warning signs include dizziness, severe headache, hallucinations, fast heartbeat and severe diarrhoea
  • low sodium levels: warning signs include headache, trouble focusing, memory problems, not thinking clearly, weakness, seizures, or losing your balance
  • bleeding in your gut: warning signs include vomiting or coughing up blood; blood in the urine; black, red, or tarry stools; bleeding from the gums; unusual vaginal bleeding; bruises that appear without a reason or that get bigger; or any bleeding that is very bad or that you cannot stop
  • thoughts about harming yourself or ending your life
  • chest pain or pressure
  • shortness of breath
  • severe dizziness or passing out
  • putting on or losing weight without trying to
  • changes in your periods such as heavy bleeding, spotting, or bleeding between periods
  • painful erections that last longer than four hours

Medical emergency! Stop taking the medicine and seek urgent medical help if you have:

A serious allergic reaction to citalopram. Warning signs include itchy skin rash; wheezing; tightness in the chest or throat; having trouble breathing or swelling of the mouth, face, lips, tongue, or throat.

Keep taking the medicine, but tell your doctor if you have any of these very common side effects:

(affects at least one in 10 people)

  • feeling sleepy and/or unable to sleep
  • dry mouth
  • sweating a lot

Keep taking the medicine, but tell your doctor if you have any of these common side effects:

(affects between 1 in 10 and 1 in 100 people)

  • upset stomach
  • dizziness
  • loss of appetite
  • feeling tired or weak
  • diarrhoea
  • shakiness
  • sweating a lot
  • yawning
